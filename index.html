Discover the region

Exploring Ronda

Perched above a dramatic gorge in the Andalusian mountains, Ronda is one of Spain's most extraordinary towns. Here are our favourite spots to make a proper trip of it.

Landmark

Puente Nuevo

Ronda's iconic 18th-century bridge spanning the 120m El Tajo gorge. The views at sunset are unforgettable.

· In town

Historic Quarter

La Ciudad (Old Town)

Wander cobbled alleyways, Arab baths, and whitewashed squares. Allow a whole morning to get properly lost.

· In town

Restaurant

Tragatá

Celebrated tapas bar from Benito Gómez. Modern Andalusian small plates — book ahead, it fills up fast.

· In town

Nature & Hiking

Sierra de las Nieves

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve on Ronda's doorstep. Ancient pinsapo fir forests, dramatic peaks, and total silence.

· 15 mins drive

Wine & Food

Ronda Wine Route

The Serranía de Ronda is an emerging wine region with high-altitude vineyards producing exceptional reds. Tours available.

· Within 20 mins

Stay

Parador de Ronda

Spectacularly positioned on the edge of the gorge, with views straight down the cliff face. The finest address in town.

· In town

The nearest airports are Málaga (1 hour drive) and Seville (1.5 hours). Both have excellent connections from the UK and Europe. There's also a scenic train from Málaga or from Seville if you'd prefer to arrive by rail — we highly recommend it. We'll be sharing more detailed travel notes closer to the date.
Ronda itself has wonderful places to stay. We recommend the Parador de Ronda for something special (book early), or Hotel Catalonia Ronda as a lovely mid-range option. There are also beautiful rural fincas in the surrounding countryside if you'd like to make a proper retreat of it. A shuttle will run between Ronda town centre and the venue on the day.
Think elegant but relaxed — light suits, blazers with linen trousers, or smart chinos for men; midi or maxi dresses, jumpsuits, or elegant separates for women. October in Ronda is warm during the day (around 22–24°C) but cools pleasantly in the evening, so a light layer is a good idea. Heels will be on terrace and grass — wedges or block heels work beautifully.
Yes — we'll be running a shuttle from Ronda town centre (departing from near the Parador) at 3:15 pm and 3:45 pm, and return shuttles will run throughout the evening. Please let us know on your RSVP if you'll need a spot so we can plan numbers. A taxi rank will also be available at the venue for later departures.
Early October in Ronda is genuinely beautiful — warm golden days (22–26°C), clear skies, and cool evenings. It's the sweet spot after the summer heat. There is a small chance of rain so we always have a contingency covered plan in place, but we're optimistic! Sunscreen during the afternoon ceremony is a good idea.
We love your children dearly, but we've chosen to make our day an adults-only celebration (18+) so that everyone — parents most of all — can truly let their hair down. We hope you understand and can arrange childcare. If this presents any difficulty please get in touch and we'll do our best to help.
