Discover the region

Ronda

Perched above a dramatic gorge in the Andalusian mountains, Ronda is one of Spain's most extraordinary towns. Here are our favourite spots to make a proper trip of it.

We've also put together a carefully curated map of our favourite places — restaurants, tapas bars, culture, and rotisserie chicken shops.

Local Secret · Street Food

Pollo Asado Shops

Ronda's rotisserie chicken shops are a local institution — whole birds slow-roasted over wood, sold by the half with bread and alioli. Look for the smoke drifting from the old town side streets. Asador El Pollo near Calle Virgen de la Paz is a favourite. Costs a few euros, tastes like a dream.

· Old town side streets

Nature & Hiking

Sierra de las Nieves

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve on Ronda's doorstep. Ancient pinsapo fir forests, dramatic peaks, and total silence.

· 15 mins drive

Wine & Food

Ronda Wine Route

The Serranía de Ronda is an emerging wine region with high-altitude vineyards producing exceptional reds. Tours available.

· Within 20 mins

Stay

Spectacularly positioned on the edge of the gorge, with views straight down the cliff face. The finest address in town.

The nearest airports are Málaga (1 hour drive) and Seville (1.5 hours). Both have excellent connections from the UK and Europe. There's also a scenic train from Málaga or from Seville if you'd prefer to arrive by rail. We'll be sharing more detailed travel notes closer to the date.
Ronda itself has wonderful places to stay. We recommend the Parador de Ronda for something special (book early), or Hotel Catalonia Ronda as a lovely mid-range option. There are also beautiful rural fincas in the surrounding countryside if you'd like to make a proper retreat of it.
